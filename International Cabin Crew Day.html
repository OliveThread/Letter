May 31st, 2025 · Doha, Qatar
Dear Yuyoung,

Today, on your first International Cabin Crew Day, I want to take a moment to write to you. Not just because the calendar calls for it, but because you deserve to hear this, and you deserve to hear it today.

One year. One extraordinary, turbulent, brave, beautiful year. When you stepped onto that first Qatar Airways flight, you chose a life of service and the world, in its unpredictable way, decided to test just how serious you were. And Yuyoung, you passed every single test.

When Doha was bombed and the city shook with fear, you were there. When tensions between the US and Iran turned the skies into something uncertain and frightening, you still showed up, strapped in, and put your passengers' comfort before your own. When you were stranded in Copenhagen, far from Doha, far from family, surrounded by grey skies and an unfamiliar city, you held yourself together with a quiet strength that most people never have to find.

And through all of it, you were also learning to call the Middle East home. A culture so different, a heat so unfamiliar, a world so far from everything you knew. That alone takes courage most people never speak of.

I want you to know: the way you carry yourself through all of this does not go unnoticed. Every passenger who sat safely in their seat, every colleague who leaned on you in a difficult moment, every journey that landed smoothly because of the calm and care you brought to it. That is your legacy, written one flight at a time.

You are doing an amazing job. Not just "good for the circumstances." Truly, genuinely, remarkable.

And on the days when the exhaustion hits, when you are 30,000 feet above a country you can't name and missing people you love, I want you to remember this: God has not taken His hand off you for a single second. Every flight you landed safely, every storm you passed through, every morning you put on that uniform again despite everything, He was there. He is with you still.

The road ahead may not always be smooth. There will be more turbulence, the kind in the sky, and the kind in life. But the same God who guided you through everything in your life will continue to light your path forward. He does not start a good work and abandon it.

Fly with confidence. Fly with faith. Fly knowing that you are exactly where you are meant to be, growing into exactly who you are meant to become. The skies are yours.

Happy International Cabin Crew Day. Here's to everything that is still to come.

"For I know the plans I have for you," declares the Lord, "plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future."
